Description


Senior Management Group of Secretary General is a forum for exchange of information and experiences amongst all the heads of departments, offices, funds and programmes of the United Nations. With over 50 people with the rank of Under Secretary General, these people wield dramatical influence over the organization, thereby raising and providing guidance to Secretary General on important cross-cutting issues.


Approved by General Assembly in 1997, the cabinet was formed as a reform proposal by the erstwhile Secretary General Kofi Annan, for levying assistance on conplex issues faced by Secretary General in policy-related matters to reforms to be introduced in the organization affecting the whole entity as one.


Generally, Senior Management Group shall meet every two weeks, to cooperate, conglomerate and analyze any situation deemed fit either by group or by the Secretary General.
